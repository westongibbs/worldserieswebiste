Historical Dates in the History of the World Series

1903

The first modern World Series game is played between the Boston Americans (Red Sox) and the Pittsburgh Pirates. This marks the beginning of the annual championship series.

1988

In Game 1 of the World Series between the Los Angeles Dodgers and the Oakland Athletics, injured Dodgers player Kirk Gibson hits a walk-off home run, considered one of the most iconic moments in baseball history.

1993

Joe Carter of the Toronto Blue Jays hits a walk-off home run in Game 6 of the World Series against the Philadelphia Phillies, securing the Blue Jays' second consecutive championship.

2004

The Boston Red Sox win their first World Series in 86 years, breaking the "Curse of the Bambino," a drought that began after they sold Babe Ruth to the New York Yankees.

2016

The Chicago Cubs win their first World Series in 108 years, defeating the Cleveland Indians in a dramatic Game 7 that ended in extra innings, ending the longest championship drought in North American professional sports history.
